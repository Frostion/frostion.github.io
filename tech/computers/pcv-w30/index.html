sony vaio pcv-w30

overview

the sony vaio pcv-w30 is the coolest computer of all time. its otherworldly, all-in-one design and crazy folding keyboard make it seem like it's from another universe (which it probably is). this is my "daily driver" machine - it's not super powerful (and can't be upgraded too much due to being an all-in-one), but it's more totally fine for my computing purposes.

much like most vaio computers, this was designed to be an all-in-one multimedia experience. the display is super crisp and bright with beautiful colors that put most other LCD monitors to shame. the built-in speakers are also fantastic - most devices' built-in speakers are painfully quiet and tinny, but these sound like good desktop computer speakers while being way smaller and built-in. they're good enough that i'll use it as a CD boombox when working on stuff around the house. it really lives up to its Video Audio Integrated Operation name (that's actually what vaio stands for).

i've upgraded this computer with 2GB of DDR-333 RAM (originally 512MB) and a 128GB SSD to replace the failing original 80GB HDD.

specifications

  • os: windows XP home sp3
  • processor: pentium 4 2.0GHz
  • graphics: SIS integrated
  • memory: 2GB DDR-333
  • storage: 128GB SSD
