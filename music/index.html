my favorite albums

Music is my favorite form of media. I've never really been into TV or films, and it's a challenge to get me to actually commit to playing a game, but I'll happily sit through entire albums at a time. I'm mostly drawn to 90s and 2000s electronic music (don't call it EDM) but will occasionally branch out into indie pop and other related genres.

Here's a list of some of my favorite albums in alphabetical order (a ✨ icon indicates my all-time favorites). Click on a thumbnail to listen to the album (usually on Bandcamp or YouTube). If you happen to like any of these albums, I highly recommend getting them on CD or vinyl (or just burn your own CD or MD if you can't find an official release). Music is best experienced on dedicated, physical media.

Astral Projection - Another World (1999)

Classic goa/psytrance from one of the most prolific psytrance groups ever. I highly recommend this if you're interested in psy but aren't sure where to start.

Barker - Utility (2019)

Punchy, sharp, molten, ambient-inspired techno. No heavy-hitting kicks or rhythms here though, just refreshing digital fluidity.

Big Bud - Infinity + Infinity (1999)

Tight and precise intelligent dnb that transports your consciousness to a Bryce-rendered raytraced digital reality.

✨ DV-i - Research + Development (2017)

Hyper-digital hyper-networked hyper-future hyper-breakbeat straight from the digital plane. Dreamcast GD-ROM shrapnel that embeds itself deep into your soul. Check out Implementation, DV-i's other EP, as well for more hyper-digital music!

✨ Every Little Thing - ELT TRANCE (2002)

Overflowing with hyper, energetic, epic supersaws and relentless pounding beats, this is my personal favorite trance album of all time, ever - it's exactly what trance is supposed to be.

The Glitch Mob - Ctrl Alt Reality (2022)

The Glitch Mob's trademark dark and punchy style infused with classic white-hot ravey jungle breaks. Classic and nostalgic, yet fresh and unique at the same time.

The Glitch Mob - Drink The Sea (2010)

Massive, dark, moody, atmospheric nighttime warehouse feels.

✨ icotec - Signal (2004)

Intelligent dnb but also trip-hop and downtempo all at the same time somehow? Absolutely top-notch cyber atmosphere that I can't even begin to figure out how to describe so just LISTEN TO IT IMMEDIATELY!!!! you're the kind of person to visit my website so you will absolutely love it

✨ Inner-Vation - Movements (1999)

A 71-minute spiritual experience masquerading as a dnb album. The song "Angels Call" in particular will take your consciousness to a different plane.

✨ Keep Shelly in Athens - In Love With Dusk / Our Own Dream (2012)

A distant nighttime thunderstorm of an album that's perfect to listen to on a rainy day - slow, introspective, somber, with just a little bit of spice sprinkled in.

✨ Ken Ishii - Jelly Tones (1995)

This is the album that get me into techno, and I still haven't found anything that tops its relentlessly industrial and computerized style. The soundtrack to a future beyond our understanding.

✨ KITCALIBER - HALCYONDAZE (2020)

NON-STOP XTREME digital hardcore breakbeat action that will absolutely melt your mind!!!! I highly recommend watching the album's music video as well.

✨ Namco Consumer Software Sound Team - R4 / Ridge Racer Type 4 / Direct Audio (1998)

Not only is it the soundtrack to one of the best racing games ever, but it's also just a fantastic album on its own! Funky, jazzy, discoy, groovy, drum'n'bassy.

Neon Indian - Era Extrana (2011)

The dreamiest of dream pop. A hot, humid summer night in a place outside of time, somewhere you don't recognize but you feel strangely nostalgic for.

✨ Onoken - Swell Strings (2003)

Uhhh this is probably one of (if not the) best electronic music albums of all time? It's got the best trance you'll ever hear, the best dnb you'll ever hear, and it's all tied together with beautiful orchestral compositions and vocal sections. I highly recommend dedicating an hour to just listening to this album from start to finish.

Paul Oakenfold - Tranceport (1998)

If you're interested in trance but don't know where to start, this is the album for you! It exposes you to a wide variety of styles and artists, so you're sure to find something that you like in here! (Also, please get the CD - the booklet is full of fantastic late 90s graphic design. You won't regret it.)

Satoshi & Makoto - CZ5000 Sounds & Sequences (2017)

Masterfully minimal soundscapes crafted solely with the beautiful, pure tones of a Casio CZ-5000 synthesizer. This is the album to play if you need something chill to relax and refresh your mind with.

✨ she - Coloris (2008)

An entire sci-fi universe contained within a single album. High-energy, high-tech, digital funk chiptune rock pop all at once. I first bought this album around when it was released over 10 years ago and it's been one of my all-time favorites ever since then - it never gets old.

Storm From The East 2 (1997)

Intelligent dnb that will nourish your digital soul. An excellent compilation of some of the best that dnb has to offer.

Trans-Central Connection (1996)

Same thing as Storm From The East - another excellent compilation of the best intelligent dnb around. Both are equally good in my opinion.

✨ Virtual Self - Virtual Self (2017)

A modern love letter to early 2000s house and trance with a glowy cyberspiritual vibe. Also, the Virtual Self website is cool as heck!
